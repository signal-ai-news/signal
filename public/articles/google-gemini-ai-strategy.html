Google's Gemini Gap

Google releases new Gemini models, but a key version is still missing

Jul 21, 2026 294 WORDS 1 TOOL LINKS TechCrunch AI

Google's Gemini Expansion

Google just dropped three new Gemini models: the 3.6 Flash, 3.5 Flash-Lite, and Flash Cyber. You'd think this would be a straightforward expansion of their AI offerings, but it's actually raising more questions than answers.

The Missing Piece

The main question on everyone's mind is: what about the Gemini 3.5 Pro? It's the version that was expected to be the real powerhouse, but it's nowhere to be found. This isn't the first time Google has skipped a beat with its Gemini releases, and it's starting to look like a pattern.

So, what does this mean for Google's overall AI strategy? Are they shifting focus away from the Pro line, or is this just a temporary detour? The company hasn't given any clear answers, leaving us to speculate about their plans.

What We Do Know

The new models do bring some impressive specs to the table. The 3.6 Flash, for example, boasts improved performance and efficiency. The 3.5 Flash-Lite is a more budget-friendly option that still packs a punch. And the Flash Cyber... well, that one's a bit of an enigma, but it's clear Google is experimenting with new approaches to AI.

What it means for you

If you're waiting for the Gemini 3.5 Pro, you'll just have to keep waiting. But if you're in the market for a new AI tool, the latest Gemini models are definitely worth a look. Just don't expect a clear answer about what's coming next – at least, not yet.

As Google continues to evolve its AI offerings, one thing is certain: the company is pushing the boundaries of what's possible with artificial intelligence. And we'll be keeping a close eye on what's next, because in the world of AI, you can't afford to stand still for a moment.
