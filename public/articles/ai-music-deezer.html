AI Music Takes Over

Deezer reveals over 50% of daily uploads are AI-generated tracks

Jul 21, 2026 488 WORDS TechCrunch AI

AI-Generated Music On The Rise

Music streaming platform Deezer has announced that more than 50% of its daily uploads are AI-generated tracks. This staggering statistic comes after the platform reported over 90,000 AI-generated tracks were uploaded daily in June.

What's Behind The Surge?

The rise of AI-generated music can be attributed to the increasing accessibility of music creation tools. With the help of AI, artists can now produce high-quality tracks without extensive musical knowledge or training. This has democratized music creation, allowing more people to produce and upload their music.

Deezer's findings also suggest that AI-generated music is becoming increasingly popular among listeners. The platform's users are embracing the unique sounds and styles that AI-generated music has to offer.

Quality And Authenticity

However, the surge in AI-generated music raises questions about the quality and authenticity of the tracks. While AI can produce music that sounds polished and professional, it often lacks the emotional depth and human touch that traditional music provides.

What it means for you

If you're a music lover, you'll likely encounter more AI-generated tracks on Deezer and other streaming platforms. This could lead to a more diverse and experimental music landscape, but it also means you may need to dig deeper to find music that resonates with you on a personal level.

The Future Of Music

As AI-generated music continues to grow in popularity, it will be interesting to see how the music industry adapts. Will AI-generated music become the new norm, or will listeners ultimately crave the unique qualities of human-created music? Only time will tell, but one thing is certain: the music landscape is changing, and AI is at the forefront of this revolution.

The Quality Problem

While AI-generated music is flooding streaming platforms, the quality varies wildly. Some AI tracks are virtually indistinguishable from human-created music, while others are clearly machine-generated with repetitive patterns and unnatural vocal inflections.

Deezer's detection system uses audio analysis algorithms to identify AI-generated content. The platform has been developing this technology since 2023, and it now catches the majority of AI uploads before they enter recommendation algorithms.

Industry Response

Other streaming platforms are watching Deezer's approach closely. Spotify has implemented similar detection mechanisms, while Apple Music has taken a more hands-off approach, relying on content ID systems instead.

The music industry is divided on how to handle AI music. Some artists see it as a threat to their livelihood, while others embrace it as a creative tool. Labels are increasingly using AI for mastering and production, even if they're hesitant about fully AI-generated tracks.

What It Means for You

For listeners, the flood of AI music means more content but potentially lower average quality. For musicians, it's both a competitive threat and a potential tool. Understanding how platforms handle AI content can help you navigate this changing landscape.
